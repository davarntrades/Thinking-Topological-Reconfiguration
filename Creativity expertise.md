## The Explanation Everyone Gets Wrong

Ask anyone why children are more creative than experts and you will hear some version of the same answer:

```
"They're not afraid to be wrong."
"They haven't been conditioned yet."
"They have beginner's mind."
"They're more playful."
"They're more curious."
"They haven't learned the rules."
```

These answers feel true. They are also all wrong.

They describe symptoms. They do not describe the geometry.

The Morrison Framework™ gives the first precise, mathematical explanation of why children outperform experts in creativity — and it has nothing to do with psychological states, fear, or innocence.

It has to do with the sign of a derivative.
